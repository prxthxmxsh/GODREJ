Everyone walks through The Grove.
Almost no one stays after dark.

It's 1.2 acres of old trees and open lawn in the middle of XLRI — lovely by day, and quietly empty every evening, because there isn't a single light. This is about giving those evenings back to campus.

02 — The idea

Add light. Change nothing else.

One small move does the work. The lawn stays open to everyone all day, and the old court finally gets used again in the evening.

A space only gets adopted if it doesn't quietly grow a bill. So we kept the running cost to a minimum: the lawn is already cared for, solar lights cover most of the new power, and students handle the bookings themselves.

Rough figures for the pitch — the point is the shape of it: almost everything here is already paid for.

06 — Before you decide

The honest bit.

The real risk

The real risk isn't money or building work — it's whether people actually show up. We could light the space and still have a quiet lawn. That's why the change is small, easily undone, and built around a path people already use. If it doesn't catch on, we've spent very little and paved nothing. If it does, the space was ready all along.
